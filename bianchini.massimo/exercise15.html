Final project: OBIKE

My final project – called OBike – is developing from the initial concept. The basic principles of my concept remained unchanged but during these months the project has been very modified and simplified. This does not mean that in the next future I could develop a kit with the same characteristics that I had originally planned. The initial concept contained a number of inaccuracies (or naive) related to the lack of pragmatic (technical and technological) knowledge and skills related to specific production processes and components. Throughout the Fab Academy I learned a lot of things especially in the field of electronics and programming. In this way, the project has been thoroughly revised to become feasible.






What will it do?

Obike is a small smart multisensors system designed primarily for the safety of the cyclist and then to develop collaborative services related to sustainable mobility in urban areas. Obike is made by two parts:

  • Hardware: a case of sticking to the bicycle equipped with a sensor for detecting the safe distance and a sensor for detecting the atmospheric CO2 level. A Bluetooth module to transmit data from the sensor to a smartphone.
  • Software: an application receives the signals from the sensors via Bluetooth and performs two actions: one generates a sound when a vehicle comes too close to the bicycle, the other download data on the level of CO2.

    What materials and components will be required?

    Below a first list (BOM) of materials and components should be used to materialize OBike. The shell, 3D printed (PLA) will contain:

  • FabKit 0.4 based on Atmega382 (this PCB could be replaced by industrial or customized microcontroller)
  • A Bluetooth module
  • An infrared sensor (less reliable) or an ultrasound sensor (more reliable)
  • A sensor for CO2 detection
  • A 3.3V Li-Po Battery


    • How much will it cost?

    The estimated cost for the construction of the kit Obike is estimated at 60 € so divided:

  • € 10 for the FabKit 0.4 components
  • 20 for CO2 and infrared/ultrasound sensors
  • 10 € for Li-Po Battery
  • 12 € for Bluetooth module
  • 20 € for 3D printing the shell (40 g PLA)


    • What parts and systems will be made? What processes will be used?

    The shell and the bike mount for the smartphone will be 3D printed.The small PCB that will host the Bluetooth Module will be designed and milled (desktop milling machine) installing all the components. The basic smartphone app will be designed and coded using Python and Kivy.


    What tasks need to be completed? What questions need to be answered?

    The FabKit 0.4 has been specifically created for the Inputs and Outputs Assignments. The PCB for the Bluetooth Module has already been designed and milled.The main questions are related to the electronics and software/app tasks. I have to improve my capabilities in these tasks.
