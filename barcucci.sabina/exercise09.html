Molding & Casting

PneuNets Bending Actuator


In this tutorial you will find the steps that I faced to design, mold and cast a soft robot, a PneuNets Bending actuator. I started to study this topic with the 3D printing module and now I am ready to prototype a more complex actuator. The final goal is to create something close to this air-muscle designed by Bijing Zhang & Francois Mangion as part of "Furl: Soft Pneumatic Pavilion" (Bartlett University):

Design

To understand and design my actuator, I followed the thorough tutorial I found on Soft Robotics Toolkit and I designed my actuator taking ispiration by the one of Pneumatic Pavilion.



Two parts are necessary to cast a silycon bening PenuNets actuator, which in total are made by three molds. You can understand the elements here, especially looking at the section. The dimensions reported are those I used for my model.



I designed first in 2D the 3 actuatuors components and then I exported in rhino the dwg file to extrude them, taking into consideration the machinable wax carving block dimensions.







Fabrication



Once the stl models were ready, I created the toolpaths for the Shopbot Alpha model milling machine. I used VCarve as postprocessor for this purpose. Below the settings I used for the three models (I used purple machinable wax):

- diameter: 2 mm
- pass depht: 1 mm
- Stepover: 40 %
- Spindle speed: 18000 rpm (to be lowered and fine tuned manually)
- feed rate: 6000 mm/min (since the machine works with in minutes units)
- plunge: 1500 mm/min (same reason)
